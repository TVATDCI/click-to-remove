Carnivorous

The largest Carnivorous dinosaur is the Spinosaurus, which is 50 feet long and weighs 20 tons. For more than 50 years, it was believed that the Tyrannosaurus rex was the largest carnivorous dinosaur. However, in 2014, the Spinosaurus was named the largest carnivorous dinosaur. The Spinosaurus lived in North Africa and was the largest predator to ever walk the Earth. Fun fact: The Spinosaurus was a vegatarian and was the first dinosaur to be adapted to an aquatic lifestyle.

Herbivorous

The largest herbivorous dinosaur is the Argentinosaurus, which is over 100 feet long and weighs 100 tons. The Argentinosaurus lived in South America during the Late Cretaceous period. Fun fact: The Argentinosaurus was the largest dinosaur to ever walk the Earth, too!

Theropods

The largest Theropod dinosaur is the Spinosaurus, which is 50 feet long and weighs 20 tons. For more than 50 years, it was believed that the Tyrannosaurus rex was the largest carnivorous dinosaur. However, in 2014, the Spinosaurus was named the largest carnivorous dinosaur. Theropods never walk the Earth. Fun fact: Theropods had know legs. So they are the first dinosaur to be adapted to an aquatic lifestyle.
